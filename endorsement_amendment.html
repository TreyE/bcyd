Amendment for Endorsement

  1. ENDORSEMENTS.

    1. During any election year, the membership of BCYD may make endorsements of candidates for office.

      1. The membership may only endorse one candidate per elected position.

      2. Any member may make a motion to endorse for a specified office.

      3. Members may not make a motion to endorse a specific candidate.

      4. A separate motion is required to endorse for each office.

    2. Endorsement shall be a four step process:

      1. At a general body meeting, a member must make a seconded motion to endorse for an office.

      2. The Executive Board shall then make a list of all candidates for the specified office to be presented at next general body meeting; it is the duty of the Executive Board to ensure that members are able to make informed decisions when voting on endorsements.

      3. At the next general body meeting, the list of candidates for the specified office must be approved unanimously by the general body.

      4. A motion to hold a vote on endorsement for the specified office may then be made:

        1. Voting shall be conducted through single transferable vote and by secret ballot.

        2. The president shall count the votes publicly under the supervision of the Secretary.

        3. A majority of voting members is required to endorse a candidate for office.
